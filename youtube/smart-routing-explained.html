Smart routing v2 explained — what's actually happening behind the scenes

Channel: Evolve · Duration: 11:24 · Published: April 19, 2026 · 7,902 views

Per-network success-rate optimization sounds vague until you see what's actually happening. We walk through the routing decision for a real charge — how Evolve picks an acquirer based on per-card-type approval rates, network token availability, and transaction shape, and how soft-decline failover works without the customer noticing.

  • 3:48 — Network tokens and why they're a routing factor

  • 8:30 — Customizing rules: when to override the model
  • 10:00 — Measuring the lift on your own traffic

Transcript highlights

"…the model isn't picking 'best acquirer overall.' It's picking 'best acquirer for this card BIN, in this currency, at this time of day, given this transaction shape.' On a Visa Brazilian co-brand at 3am UTC, the answer might be different from a US Mastercard at noon. That granularity is where the 1–3% lift comes from…"

"…soft-decline failover is the unsung hero. About 0.7% of charges that get a soft decline (issuer says 'try again') succeed on a second attempt through a different acquirer, in the same authorization window. Customer never knows it happened…"
